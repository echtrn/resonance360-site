Clinical Services

MSK Radiology &
Interventions

Subspecialty MSK expertise for complex imaging and image-guided intervention. Open to clinician referrals and patient-direct enquiries. Radiologic Clinic, Mount Elizabeth Novena Hospital, Singapore.

A considered, boutique service.
Not a high-volume read factory.

Second opinion cases are reviewed for suitability before acceptance. Consultations take place in person at the Radiologic Clinic, Mount Elizabeth Novena Hospital - please bring digital copies of your imaging on a disc or USB. This is a deliberate approach: every case accepted is one Dr Tham has the capacity and context to engage with properly.

Two referral pathways. Second opinions are open to both referring clinicians and patients seeking a direct specialist read. Clinician-referred cases carry the full benefit of clinical context and history. Patient-direct enquiries are welcome, but the scope of the opinion will be more limited - imaging alone tells only part of the story. Image-guided interventions remain referral-based.

Service 01

MSK Radiology
Second Opinions

Expert subspecialty review of complex or equivocal musculoskeletal imaging. For cases where the primary read is uncertain, where findings don't fit the clinical picture, or where a specialist interpretation is needed before committing to surgery or further workup.

Each opinion is a detailed subspecialty report - not a restatement of findings, but a considered read that helps move a case forward. Consultations are in person at the Radiologic Clinic, Mount Elizabeth Novena Hospital. Please bring digital copies of all relevant imaging. All cases are reviewed for suitability prior to acceptance.

Accepted from Referring clinicians (orthopaedic surgeons, spine surgeons, rheumatologists, sports medicine physicians, neurologists, GPs) · Patients seeking a direct specialist read (scope of opinion is more limited without clinical referral context)

What we cover

Spine

  • Cervical, thoracic and lumbar disc disease
  • Spinal canal and foraminal stenosis
  • Spondylolisthesis and instability
  • Post-operative spine assessment
  • Inflammatory and infective spinal conditions

Joints

  • Shoulder - rotator cuff, labrum, AC joint
  • Hip - labrum, femoroacetabular impingement, avascular necrosis
  • Knee - menisci, cruciate ligaments, cartilage, bone
  • Ankle and foot - tendons, ligaments, osteochondral lesions
  • Wrist and hand - TFCC, intrinsic ligaments, tendons
  • Elbow - ligamentous and tendon pathology

Soft Tissue & Other

  • Muscle tears and myotendinous injuries
  • Tendinopathy and tendon tears
  • Soft tissue masses - characterisation and staging input
  • Sports-related and overuse injuries

Service 02

MSK & Spine
Interventions

Image-guided procedures for musculoskeletal and spinal conditions, performed under fluoroscopic or ultrasound guidance. Precise, targeted, and planned in full clinical context - not a standalone procedure service, but an extension of subspecialty MSK practice.

All interventions are referral-based. Cases are reviewed prior to procedure to confirm indication, imaging correlation, and procedural plan. Referring clinicians receive a procedure report and are kept in the loop throughout.

For Orthopaedic surgeons · Spine surgeons · Rheumatologists · Pain specialists · Sports medicine physicians · General practitioners

Procedures offered

Peripheral Joint & Soft Tissue

  • Intra-articular joint injections
  • Tendon and peritendinous injections
  • Bursa injections
  • Ganglion aspiration and injection
  • Perineural injections for nerve entrapment

Spine

  • Epidural steroid injections (interlaminar and transforaminal)
  • Facet joint injections (cervical, thoracic, lumbar)
  • Medial branch blocks
  • Selective nerve root blocks
  • Sacroiliac joint injections

Specialist Procedures

  • Vertebral augmentation (kyphoplasty / vertebroplasty)
  • Radiofrequency ablation for osteoid osteoma
  • Bone biopsy - image-guided, musculoskeletal

How It Works

No layers. No queues.
Every case reviewed before acceptance.

Whether you are a referring clinician or a patient seeking a direct specialist read, the process is the same - direct, considered, and handled personally.

Step 01

Make an enquiry

Email hello@resonance360.com with your clinical question. Clinicians: include relevant clinical history and the specific question you need answered. Patients: describe your situation and the imaging you have had done.

Step 02

Submit your scans

Once your case is accepted, submit your imaging - either as a digital link or physically to the Radiologic Clinic, Mount Elizabeth Novena Hospital - along with a completed clinical questionnaire.

Step 03

Your imaging will be reviewed

Your submitted scans will be reviewed by Dr Tham and a detailed second opinion report formulated. A referral to an appropriate clinical specialist may also be recommended if necessary.

Ready to make an enquiry?

Clinician referrals and patient-direct enquiries both welcome for second opinions. Reach out via the contact form or email directly. All cases are reviewed before acceptance.
